**Bedatty Engineering** is a **personal** GitHub organization: homelab-style projects, experiments, and shared tooling. Interaction here is usually low-volume, but **respect still matters**—in issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other public space under this org.

This document adapts the **Contributor Covenant** to that reality: one maintainer, best-effort moderation, and the same baseline expectations you would want in any public technical forum.

## Our pledge

We as visitors, contributors, and the maintainer pledge to make participation **harassment-free** for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an **open, welcoming, and healthy** space, even when we disagree technically.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people  
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences  
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback  
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing when mistakes affect others, and learning from it  
- Focusing on what helps the **project and readers**, not on winning an argument  

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind  
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks  
- Public or private **harassment**  
- Publishing others’ **private information** (e.g. physical address, private email) without explicit permission  
- Other conduct that would reasonably be considered **inappropriate in a professional community**  

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to **all repositories and public interaction spaces** under the [Bedatty-Engineering](https://github.com/Bedatty-Engineering) organization on GitHub (issues, pull requests, discussions, org profile, and related public representation).

It does **not** replace each repository’s **license** or **security** policy—see [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) for vulnerabilities.

## Enforcement responsibilities

The **org maintainer** ([@bedatty](https://github.com/bedatty)) is responsible for clarifying these standards and taking **fair, proportionate** action when behavior is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

The maintainer may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and may explain moderation decisions when it is safe and reasonable to do so.

There is **no dedicated moderation team** and **no SLA**—responses follow the same “spare time” expectation described in [`SUPPORT.md`](SUPPORT.md).

## Reporting

**Do not** use the security disclosure channel for general conduct reports (see [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md)).

To report a Code of Conduct concern:

- **Preferred:** contact **Lucas C. Bedatty** via the options on [**github.com/bedatty**](https://github.com/bedatty) (e.g. profile contact links, if any).  
- **Email:** **report@bedatty.com** *(use this label in the subject line, e.g. `[CoC]`, so it can be filtered)*.  

Reports will be reviewed **as soon as practical**, with **privacy for the reporter** respected where possible. Retaliation against good-faith reports is not acceptable.

## Enforcement guidelines

The maintainer may use these **guidelines** (not rigid steps) depending on severity, context, and capacity:

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Minor lapse (e.g. rude tone, off-topic flare-up).  

**Response:** Private message or short clarification; ask for adjustment going forward.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** Clear violation or repeated minor issues.  

**Response:** Explicit warning; continued behavior may lead to restricted interaction (e.g. locked thread, request to disengage).

### 3. Temporary restriction

**Impact:** Serious or sustained disruption.  

**Response:** Temporary block from org/repo interaction on GitHub (or equivalent), for a defined period, with a short explanation when appropriate.

### 4. Permanent removal

**Impact:** Harassment, hate directed at people or groups, doxxing, or a **pattern** of abuse after warnings.  

**Response:** Permanent ban from org spaces on GitHub, and/or deletion of abusive content.

**GitHub’s own tools** (block, report abuse to GitHub) remain available to anyone for platform-level issues.
